1890
🌐 Global

The Right to Privacy

Warren & Brandeis, Harvard Law Review

  • Samuel Warren and Louis Brandeis published the foundational essay coining the phrase "right to be let alone."
  • Argued that the law must protect individuals from undue media exposure as technology advanced.
  • Set the philosophical bedrock for all future privacy legislation worldwide.
1948
🌐 Global

Universal Declaration of Human Rights

United Nations — Article 12

  • Article 12 explicitly states: no one shall be subjected to arbitrary interference with privacy, family, home or correspondence.
  • Elevated privacy from a legal concept to a fundamental human right on the world stage.
  • Became the moral anchor for subsequent international privacy treaties and national constitutions.
1974
🌐 Global

U.S. Privacy Act

United States Federal Law

  • Governed how U.S. federal agencies collect, maintain, use, and disseminate personally identifiable information.
  • Granted citizens the right to access and request corrections to their federal records.
  • One of the first laws to translate privacy philosophy into enforceable government obligations.
1995
🌐 Global
💻 Digital Age

EU Data Protection Directive

European Union — Directive 95/46/EC

  • Established the EU's first comprehensive framework for protecting personal data across member states.
  • Introduced key principles: purpose limitation, data minimization, and individual rights to access and erasure.
  • Became a global template influencing data protection laws on every continent.
2012
🇵🇭 Philippine

Republic Act 10173

Data Privacy Act of 2012 — Philippines

  • The Philippines' landmark legislation protecting the privacy of individuals' personal information in information and communications systems.
  • Established the National Privacy Commission (NPC) as the country's data protection authority.
  • Imposed obligations on Personal Information Controllers and Processors aligned with international standards.
2012
🇵🇭 Philippine

Republic Act 10175

Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 — Philippines

  • Criminalized online offenses including illegal access, data interference, cybersquatting, and online libel.
  • Complemented the Data Privacy Act by addressing criminal liability for data breaches and unauthorized access.
  • Controversial for its cyberlibel provisions, sparking national debate on free speech vs. online accountability.
2016
🌐 Global
💻 Digital Age

General Data Protection Regulation

European Union — GDPR (Effective 2018)

  • The world's most comprehensive and enforceable data protection law, replacing the 1995 Directive.
  • Introduced the "right to be forgotten," explicit consent requirements, and fines up to 4% of global annual turnover.
  • Its extraterritorial scope means any entity handling EU citizens' data must comply, reshaping global tech industry practices.
2016
🇵🇭 Philippine

IRR of RA 10173 Implemented

Implementing Rules & Regulations — NPC Philippines

  • The National Privacy Commission officially implemented the Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) of the Data Privacy Act.
  • Required all government agencies and private companies handling personal data to register with the NPC and appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO).
  • Mandated breach notification within 72 hours — aligning the Philippines with international best practices.
2018
🌐 Global
💻 Digital Age

Cambridge Analytica Scandal

Facebook / Meta — Global Data Crisis

  • Up to 87 million Facebook profiles were harvested without consent and used to build psychographic voter targeting models.
  • Triggered global legislative momentum — from the U.S. Senate hearings to accelerated GDPR enforcement actions.
  • Became the defining moment proving that personal data is a political weapon, galvanizing public demand for stronger privacy laws.
2020
🌐 Global
💻 Digital Age

California Consumer Privacy Act

CCPA / CPRA — United States

  • Granted California residents the right to know, delete, and opt out of the sale of their personal information.
  • Inspired by GDPR but tailored for the American market — considered the strongest U.S. state-level privacy law.
  • Set a de facto national standard given California's role as the home of major tech companies.
2022
🇵🇭 Philippine

NPC Circular 2022-01

National Privacy Commission — Philippines

  • Issued mandatory guidelines on data breach management and notification, increasing accountability for personal information controllers.
  • Strengthened enforcement with updated procedures on how companies must respond, contain, and report data breaches.
  • Aligned the Philippines more closely with global standards including GDPR's 72-hour breach notification rule.
2024
🌐 Global
💻 Digital Age

EU AI Act

European Union — World's First AI Law

  • The world's first comprehensive legal framework regulating artificial intelligence, with a risk-based classification system.
  • Prohibits biometric mass surveillance, AI manipulation, and social scoring — directly intersecting with personal privacy rights.
  • Sets the stage for a new era where privacy law must encompass not just data storage but algorithmic decision-making.

Looking Ahead

What's Next in Privacy?

Our predictions for the future frontier of data protection law, shaped by emerging technologies and shifting societal values.

🤖

AI Accountability Laws

Following the EU AI Act, countries worldwide — including the Philippines — will enact laws requiring algorithmic transparency, explainability, and human oversight for automated decisions affecting individuals.

Est. 2025–2027
🇵🇭

Philippine Digital Economy Privacy Act

An anticipated amendment or successor to RA 10173 that addresses e-commerce platforms, digital financial services, and social media data — modernizing the 2012 framework for the age of super-apps.

Est. 2025–2026
🧬

Biometric & Genomic Privacy

As DNA testing, facial recognition, and health wearables proliferate, specialized laws protecting biometric and genetic data will emerge — the most sensitive personal data imaginable.

Est. 2026–2030
🌐

Global Privacy Treaty

Momentum is building at the UN for a binding international convention on data privacy — a "Paris Agreement" for personal data that would hold all nations to minimum protection standards.

Est. 2028–2035
🔗

Decentralized Identity Rights

With blockchain-based identity systems emerging, new legal frameworks will define ownership and portability of digital identities — giving citizens control over their credentials without relying on tech giants.

Est. 2027–2032
🧠

Neurorights Legislation

Chile became the first country to constitutionally protect mental data. As brain-computer interfaces advance, neurorights — protecting the privacy of your thoughts — will become a mainstream legal frontier.

Est. 2030+
